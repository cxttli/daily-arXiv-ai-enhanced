Abstract: Root-MUSIC is a spectral estimation algorithm that approximates the unknown signal frequencies by constructing a high-degree polynomial and finding a subset of roots which are closest to the complex unit circle. Previous works found asymptotic expectation formulas for the performance of Root-MUSIC under the implicit assumption that the aforementioned root selection criterion does not select extraneous roots -- those which are unrelated to the correct parameters. The main mathematical insight of this paper is a geometric property of the Root-MUSIC polynomial: its correct roots are highly stable to noise while its extraneous roots must lie outside of an annulus.
